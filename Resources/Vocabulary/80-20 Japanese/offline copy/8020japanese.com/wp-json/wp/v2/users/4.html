What You'll Learn

Everything covered in 80/20 Japanese, chapter by chapter.

80/20 Japanese provides in-depth explanations and loads of exercises to help you master the most important Japanese grammar concepts. You'll learn all about:

  • Particles and sentence structure
  • All major verb conjugations
  • The te-form (and over a dozen different ways to use it)
  • Adjectives and adverbs
  • Numbers and counters
  • Time expressions
  • Complex sentence formation
  • ... and a whole lot more

But it's not just about what you learn - it's how you learn it.

The key to the effectiveness of 80/20 Japanese is the logical order in which the lessons are presented.

By teaching everything in the right order, 80/20 Japanese ensures that every new concept and sentence pattern you learn makes total sense.

In total, 80/20 Japanese covers more than 100 topics over 12 chapters. This can be broken into four parts:

1. The Japanese crash course

The first four chapters are designed to achieve two main things:

  1. Build a solid foundation
  2. Get you conversational as quickly as possible

It's vital to get the basics right from the beginning. Having a proper framework that you can apply to everything you learn makes it much easier to make sense of each new concept - even the more advanced ones. 

Of course, getting the basics right is no good if it's all just impractical theory.

We learn faster and more effectively by doing, which is why Chapter 4 in particular is all about giving you practical knowledge that will allow you to actually hold a meaningful conversation.

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the first four chapters:

Chapter 1: The Sounds of Japanese

Above all else, if you want to communicate in Japanese, you need to be able to recognize and pronounce the sounds that make up each word.

Chapter 1 walks you through all the unique sounds of Japanese so you can be sure that people understand you when you speak.

Chapter 2: Intro to Japanese Grammar

Next, you will learn the fundamental structure of Japanese sentences, with a particular focus on the particle system. Doing this early is the key to avoiding getting stuck in beginner mode for way too long.

See, most books and courses skip this vital step, and instead start with specific phrases and sentence patterns. The result is that you end up knowing how to say a bunch of random things, but have no idea why they make sense, or how you can deviate from the standard pattern to say something that's... you know... actually useful.

By giving you a better understanding of how Japanese sentences and particles work right from the beginning, 80/20 Japanese helps you build a solid foundation that will apply to everything you ever learn about Japanese grammar.

Chapter 3: General Characteristics of Japanese

Adding to your understanding of how Japanese functions, Chapter 3 covers the essential cultural understanding you need to speak Japanese effectively.

These are easy but important lessons that are applicable every time you use Japanese.

Chapter 4: The Essentials

This chapter is focused on getting you up to speed with conversational Japanese, fast. It introduces a variety of concepts that you can immediately use in real life situations, including:

  • More particles
  • Essential verb tenses needed to talk about the past, present and future
  • How to talk about things you like and want
  • How to ask questions
  • Many more useful words and expressions

2. Master the core building blocks

Now that you have a solid base to work from, it's time to look closer at each of the major pieces of the grammatical puzzle.

Over these next five chapters, youll develop the ability to properly use and combine the core building blocks, allowing you to speak and understand Japanese with greater flexibility and precision.

Chapter 5: Numbers and Counters

Here, you will learn how to count from 1 to 100 million and beyond.

More importantly, you'll learn how to use numbers in sentences by converting them into counters so you can, for example, count your friends as people instead of, say, birds or sheets of paper.

This chapter introduces some of the most common counters, and shows you how to use all counters in a sentence.

Chapter 6: Expressing Time

This chapter covers everything you need to know to talk about the three main aspects of time:

  1. Timing = When things are done
  2. Period = How long things are done for
  3. Frequency = How often things are done

For each, you'll learn all the essential counters and expressions, and how to use them in sentences.

Chapter 7: Adjectives, Nouns and Adverbs

Here you will learn all the different ways to conjugate adjectives (past tense, negative, etc.), as well as the different ways these can be used in sentences, both polite and informal.

We'll also apply the lessons about adjectives to "noun + desu" sentences, and discuss in detail everything you need to know about adverbs.

Chapter 8: Verbs

The main verb tenses, plus some useful verb-based expressions, are introduced in the polite form in Chapter 4.

This chapter goes into more depth on all things verb-related, including the main verb types, informal verb forms of each tense, the potential (can do) form, and more.

Chapter 9: The Te-Form

The te-form of verbs is insanely useful - so much so that it gets its own chapter.

Here you will learn how to convert all verbs (and adjectives) into the te-form, as well as the most useful ways to apply it.

This includes things like the "is doing" form for describing things happening right now, commands and requests, and linking multiple actions together in a sequence (eg. I did this, then that).

A dozen more te-form expressions are introduced later in Chapter 12.

3. Add complexity and depth

With the core components of the language covered, it's time to step it up a notch.

These next two chapters will expand your range of expression substantially, and set up to easily learn any new concept the language might throw at you.

Chapter 10: The Magic of Noun Phrases

This chapter really takes you to the next level. It teaches you all about noun phrases, which have a wide range of uses and give you the ability to talk about things in far greater detail.

For example, instead of saying something simple such as, I ate lunch in Shinjuku with my friends, noun phrases allow you to say much more interesting things like, I ate lunch at a new restaurant in Shinjuku with some friends that I met at high school.

Noun phrases are incredibly powerful, yet fairly simple to put together, especially in Japanese. This chapter covers them in detail.

Chapter 11: Particles in More Depth

As a key building block of the Japanese language, particles are of course covered right from Chapter 2, with more added along the way as needed. This chapter provides a far more detailed look.

The lessons in this chapter are not essential to being able to speak Japanese, but they will give you a deeper understanding of how different particles work. This will help you avoid the more subtle mistakes that even some advanced Japanese speakers sometimes make.

This chapter also answers one of the biggest questions that even confuses people who have been studying Japanese for years the difference between the particles wa and ga.

4. Learn a boat-load of useful expressions

By this point, you will have a very good understanding of Japanese.

This final chapter simply builds on everything you know by introducing 40+ useful expressions, all explained with examples in the context of everything learnt throughout the rest of the book.

Chapter 12: Lots and Lots of Expressions

This chapter is broken into five parts:

12.1 Very Useful Expressions

A few of the most useful expressions not introduced in earlier chapters.

12.2 Conditional Expressions

Several different ways of saying the equivalent of "if..." and "when...".

12.3 Te-Form Expressions

Adding to the most important te-form expressions covered in Chapter 9, this section introduces 12 more expressions that make use of the te-form.

12.4 The Passive Voice

To talk like a Japanese person, you need to be able to use indirect language. This section will show one of the most important ways to do that, with detailed lessons on the passive voice, a verb form that turns a sentence like, "He saw me", into, "I was seen by him".

12.5 Other Expressions

Another 24 common expressions - enough to get you to approximately JLPT N4 level.

Do I ultimately want to be able to read and write Japanese?

If so, the Kana + Kanji Edition will probably be better in the long run. If, however, you are mainly only interested in learning to speak and understand spoken Japanese, the Romaji Edition will be much easier to work with as you will have one less (very large) obstacle in your way.

Will 80/20 Japanese help me with the JLPT?

80/20 Japanese was not written specifically for the JLPT, but it will give you a very solid understanding of the fundamental grammar concepts that are tested - either directly or indirectly - in the exams.

The biggest difference between the different levels of the JLPT is quantity - primarily the number of words and expressions you can understand and interpret in context. 80/20 Japanese focuses on ensuring your quality of understanding is high, because that gives you a strong base to build everything else up on. Quantity is important too, but a good base makes it much easier to learn new words and expressions so that you can actually use them, which also helps with memorization.

Bottom line, if you learn everything in 80/20 Japanese and nothing else, you should be able to pass N4 comfortably, depending on your listening ability. To go from there to N3 and beyond, you would likely need other resources targeted specifically at those exams, but this should be much easier than if you studied only what was necessary to pass N4.
