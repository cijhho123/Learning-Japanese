物 animal / thing

牛 (cow) + (thing)
★★★★★
8 strokes
COCK
This is one of the most useful kanji. But it's also one of the most disgraceful kanji when it comes to using KUNyomi in random jukugo. Like 50% of the time it'll use MONO instead of the onyomi. And even when it DOES use the onyomi, there's two onyomi which are ALSO used randomly.

Fuck!

On the good side, however, most of the 物 jukugo are so common that you'll pick them up in no time.

Also worth mentioning, 物 refers to a physical thing, but the other 'thing' word (事; koto) refers more to actions or scenarios, states of mind, etc.

Onyomi

BUTSU / MOTSU

I refuse to knock BOOTS with MOST animals. Even your mom.

Mnemonic

A cow has a ribcage, therefore it is an animal.

kimono. ★★★★☆ KUNKUN

plant ★★★★☆

荷物(にもつ)

luggage ★★★★☆ FP
荷 (luggage) + 物 (animal / thing) = 荷物 (luggage)

luggage or belongings. Also, cargo (if you're a ship's captain). Also, emotional baggage, or a metaphorical burden
登場人物(とうじょうじんぶつ)

character ★★★☆☆
登場 (enter the stage) + 人 (person) + 物 (animal / thing) = 登場人物 (character)

a character in a book, play, novel, movie, etc.
本物(ほんもの)

the real thing, not a counterfeit. ★★★☆☆ KUNKUN
本 (book / the real) + 物 (animal / thing) = 本物 (the real thing, not a counterfeit.)

博物館(はくぶつかん)

museum ★★★☆☆
博 (museum　/extensive) + 物 (animal / thing) + 館 (big hall) = 博物館 (museum)

a natural history museum or aerospace museum. (fine-art museums are 美術館　（びじゅつかん））
禁物(きんもつ)

taboo or forbidden thing ★☆☆☆☆
禁 (prohibition) + 物 (animal / thing) = 禁物 (taboo or forbidden thing )
