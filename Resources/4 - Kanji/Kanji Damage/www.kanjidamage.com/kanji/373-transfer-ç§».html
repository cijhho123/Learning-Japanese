移 transfer

(wheat) + 多 (many)
★★☆☆☆
11 strokes

Onyomi

I

Mnemonic

I transfer wheat to many locations, to feed many people

Kunyomi

( を　or に ) うつ＊す broad meaning! A transfer or shift - as in, climate change, moving to a new house, transmitting a cold, yawns that are 'catching', fire spreading to the next building, or moving a conversation on to the next subject. These are all 移す！
★★★☆☆
( が ) うつ＊る something or someone gets transferred
★★★☆☆

Jukugo

移植(いしょく) する

organ transplant ★☆☆☆☆
移 (transfer) + 植 (plant) = 移植 (organ transplant)

移民(いみん)

immigrant ★☆☆☆☆
移 (transfer) + 民 (folk) = 移民 (immigrant )

immigrant (only applies to people who became Japanese citizens and generally their families live here for generations)(i.e. not you)

Countries like America, UK, or Brazil are 移民国　（いみんこく- immigrant countries), built by immigrants. Japan, like most of the world, still thinks of citizenship as blood and DNA.

So if you're coming from the USA, remember your open-minded point-of-view is a small minority!
