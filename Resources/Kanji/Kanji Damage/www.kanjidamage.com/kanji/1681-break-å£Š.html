壊 break

土 (earth) + (UFO) + 衣 (cloth)
★★★☆☆
16 strokes

Onyomi

KAI

That KITE-eating tree broke my KITE!

Mnemonic

The cloth fell from the UFO and broke when it hit the earth.

Kunyomi

( を ) こわ＊す I smash something , I break something.
★★★☆☆
( が ) こわ＊れる something doesn't work anymore: your TV, your PSP, your dishwasher. Call the repair-person!
★★★☆☆

Jukugo

壊れ物(こわれもの)

fragile thing ★★★☆☆
壊 (break) + 物 (animal / thing) = 壊れ物 (fragile thing)

崩壊(ほうかい)

collapse ★☆☆☆☆
崩 (collapse) + 壊 (break) = 崩壊 (collapse)

a collapse- said of a building, but also (figuratively) like 'The collapse of communism.'
破壊(はかい)

Destruction. ★☆☆☆☆
破 (rend) + 壊 (break) = 破壊 ( Destruction.)

Destruction. Ususaly used about bombs, or 'President Bush destroyed the economy,'. . . pretty much the same circumstances you'd use the English word. (BOOBOO: you can't use する with this one, for some reason. It's one of those nouns you can't verb)
