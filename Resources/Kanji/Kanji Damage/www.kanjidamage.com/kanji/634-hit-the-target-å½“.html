当 hit the target

(t.c.f.b.b.y.e.s.i.y.l.) + (snout)
★★★★★
6 strokes
this can always be used literally OR figuratively, just like in English.

Onyomi

TOU

I hit the target of your TOE. The weapon is this sputum.

Mnemonic

The three cute baby birds hit the pig right on the snout.

Kunyomi

( を ) あ＊てる I hit it, I guess correctly, (telling someone to guess is :当てて御覧！（あててごらん！））
★★★★★
( が ) あ＊たる to hit (the nail on the head), to correspond to something exactly.To win (a prize)
★★★★★

Jukugo

本当(ほんとう) に

for realz. ★★★★★
本 (book / the real) + 当 (hit the target) = 本当 (for realz.)

適当(てきとう) な

appropriate ★★★★☆ JERK
適 (suitable) + 当 (hit the target) = 適当 (appropriate)

appropriate, as in an appropriate gift. However, 適当　also means irresponsible! Doing something without considering the consequences. 適当な人間だ！ “He's irresponsible. He'll say anything that pops into his head.”
当然(とうぜん) の

natural, obvious ★★★☆☆
当 (hit the target) + 然 (nature) = 当然 (natural, obvious)

just, natural, deserved - although it's often used like 'That's obvious, isn't it?'
当日(とうじつ) に

that day . . . ★★☆☆☆
当 (hit the target) + 日 (sun, day) = 当日 (that day . . .)
